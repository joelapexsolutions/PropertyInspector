Step-by-Step Guide

Your Complete Home Buying Journey

A 10-step roadmap to buying your dream property with confidence — avoiding costly surprises and making informed decisions every step of the way.

Did you know? 70% of South African homeowners face unexpected repair costs within their first year of ownership, often exceeding R50,000. These surprises stem from skipping crucial steps in the buying process. This guide ensures you make informed decisions, understand the true costs, and avoid financial stress after moving in.
1

Know Your Budget

Get Pre-Approved for a Home Loan. Visit your bank to obtain a pre-approval certificate. They'll assess your income, expenses, and credit profile to determine your maximum loan amount. This crucial step gives you a realistic budget and strengthens your negotiating position when making offers. Pre-approval typically takes 1–3 days and shows sellers you're a serious buyer.

2

Calculate Your True Costs

Understand the Full Financial Picture. Your home loan is just one part of the equation. Use the Home Buyers Guide SA Calculator to determine your once-off costs — transfer duties, attorney fees, and bond registration. These typically range from R30,000 to R80,000+ depending on property price. Factor in monthly costs like rates, levies, insurance, and maintenance too.

Use Free Cost Calculator
3

Search Smart

Find Properties Within Your Budget. Browse Property24, Private Property, and other listing sites for homes aligned with your approved loan amount. Create a shortlist of 5–10 properties that meet your must-have criteria: location, bedrooms, size, and features. Remember to account for your deposit (typically 10–20% of purchase price) when setting your search parameters.

4

Research the Property

Know What You're Really Buying. Before viewing, research each property's true market value, crime statistics, nearby amenities, and area trends. This intel helps you spot overpriced listings and red flags. Access free insights from Standard Bank's Home Services Property Guide or invest in detailed Lightstone reports (R200–R500). Understanding whether a R1.5M asking price is justified for a R1M property helps you negotiate wisely.

Standard Bank Property Guide
5

Prepare Like a Pro

Set Yourself Up for Success. Download the Home Buyers Guide SA app and add your shortlisted properties. Set viewing appointments and review the app's comprehensive checklist covering structural, electrical, plumbing, and cosmetic aspects. Preparation ensures you ask the right questions and spot problems estate agents might overlook.

6

View with Confidence

Conduct a Thorough Assessment. During viewings, use the Home Buyers Guide SA app as your guide. It walks you through every room and outdoor space, prompting you to check critical elements: foundation cracks, roof condition, plumbing function, electrical safety, and damp issues. Take photos of concerns and document everything.

Not sure if something is serious? Simply snap pictures of questionable issues — wall cracks, roof damage, plumbing concerns — and show them to professionals for free opinions. Many builders, plumbers, or electricians can identify major problems just from a photo, giving you expert insights at zero cost before committing to a purchase.

7

Compare and Decide

Make an Informed Choice. Review assessment reports for all viewed properties side-by-side. Compare rating scores across categories, monthly cost estimates, required repairs, and how each property meets your expectations. The data-driven approach removes emotion from decision-making and highlights the best value option.

8

Negotiate Strategically

Use Evidence to Your Advantage. Armed with your detailed assessment report, make an informed offer. If you identified R100,000 worth of necessary repairs — roof, plumbing, electrical — use this evidence to negotiate a lower purchase price or request seller repairs before transfer. Your thorough documentation demonstrates you're a serious buyer who's done their homework.

9

Secure Your Financing

Finalize Your Home Loan. Once your Offer to Purchase is accepted, formally apply for your home loan. Submit all required documentation promptly: ID, proof of income, bank statements (3 months), and credit consent. The bank will conduct a property valuation to confirm the purchase price is reasonable. Loan approval typically takes 1–4 weeks.

10

Move In and Maintain

Start Your Homeownership Journey Right. After bond registration and transfer (typically 8–12 weeks), collect your keys and move in. Conduct a final walk-through using the Home Buyers Guide SA app to create a baseline condition report. Consider hiring a professional inspector (R2,500–R5,000) for a comprehensive assessment. This report becomes your maintenance roadmap, prioritising tasks and budgeting repairs over the next 1–3 years.

Why This Process Matters

Following these 10 steps transforms your home buying experience from stressful and uncertain to confident and informed. You'll avoid the shock of discovering R50,000+ in unexpected repairs after moving in, negotiate better prices based on actual property conditions, and maintain the joy of homeownership instead of experiencing buyer's remorse. Knowledge is your best investment protection.
