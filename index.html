The Hidden Water Damage

Cost: R85,000

Thabo bought his dream home in Johannesburg. The freshly painted walls looked perfect during viewing. Two months after moving in, heavy rains revealed extensive roof leaks and hidden damp. The previous owners had painted over water stains.

What it cost: R85,000 for roof repairs, ceiling replacement, and mould remediation
App guides you to check for fresh paint, inspect ceilings for stains, and ask about damp history

The Boundary Wall Collapse

Cost: R145,000

Michael bought a property with a beautiful high boundary wall. Six months later, after heavy rains, 20 meters of wall collapsed. His insurance claim was denied - the wall wasn't built to municipal standards and lacked proper drainage. The previous owner had built it without approved plans.

What it cost: R145,000 to demolish unsafe sections, obtain approval, and rebuild to code
Checklist prompts you to ask about building compliance certificates and approved plans

The Body Corporate Debt Trap

Cost: R250,000+

Mpho bought a sectional title apartment without researching the body corporate. She later discovered the complex owed R2.5 million for urgent repairs. Her levy immediately jumped from R1,500 to R4,200 monthly, plus a special levy of R45,000.

What it cost: R250,000+ in special levies and increased monthly costs
Assessment includes critical questions about body corporate finances and pending repairs

The Foundation Failure

Cost: R180,000

John noticed small cracks but was assured they were "just settlement." Six months after purchase, doors wouldn't close, floors sloped noticeably, and an engineer revealed serious foundation subsidence requiring underpinning.

What it cost: R180,000 for foundation repairs and structural work
App teaches you to identify serious vs. cosmetic cracks and when to get structural assessments

The Illegal Extension

Cost: R95,000 + Legal Risk

Lerato loved the extended living area during viewing. After purchase, she discovered the extension had no building plans approval. The municipality issued a compliance notice threatening demolition or requiring expensive retrospective approval.

What it cost: R95,000 for engineer reports, plans, and approval fees
Viewing checklist prompts you to ask about building approvals and alterations

The Geyser Disaster

Cost: R78,000

Nomsa moved into her new home in Pretoria. Three months later, the 15-year-old geyser burst, flooding two bedrooms and damaging ceilings, carpets, and electronics. Her insurance claim was rejected - the geyser showed years of neglect with no maintenance records.

What it cost: R78,000 for new geyser, water damage repairs, and contents
App prompts questions about geyser age, maintenance history, and drip tray condition

Essential House Viewing Guide - What to Look For

Expert tips to maximize every property viewing in South Africa

Before the Viewing

  • Research area crime statistics and nearby amenities
  • Check municipal rates and levies online
  • Prepare questions about property history
  • Download Property Inspector checklist app
  • Review common red flags to watch for
  • Charge your phone for photos during viewing

What to Look For During Viewing

  • Test all taps, lights, switches - don't assume they work
  • Check for water damage, damp patches, and mould
  • Inspect roof condition from outside
  • Test water pressure in all bathrooms
  • Look for cracks in walls and foundations
  • Document everything with photos during viewing
  • Use app tooltips to know what to inspect

Critical Questions to Ask

  • Why is the property being sold?
  • Age of electrical wiring, plumbing, and geyser?
  • Any building alterations without municipal approval?
  • Average monthly utility costs?
  • Any known defects or past insurance claims?
  • For sectional title: body corporate financial health?
  • When was roof last repaired?

Red Flags - Walk Away or Negotiate Hard

  • Fresh paint covering wall cracks or water stains
  • Musty smells indicating mould or damp
  • Sloping or uneven floors (foundation issues)
  • Old electrical panels or exposed wiring
  • Rushed viewings or seller pressure tactics
  • Reluctance to answer questions
  • Recent "quick fixes" that look poorly done
  • For complexes: high vacancy rates
