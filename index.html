Essential Property Viewing Guide

Expert tips to maximize every property viewing

Before the Viewing

  • Research area crime statistics and property prices on Property24
  • Check municipal rates, levies, and assessments online
  • Prepare questions about property history and ownership
  • Charge your phone for photos and note-taking
  • Download Property Inspector app and create property profile
  • Review common red flags so you know what to watch for

During the Viewing

  • Test all taps, lights, switches, and appliances - don't assume they work
  • Check for water damage, damp patches, and mould (especially after rain)
  • Inspect roof condition from outside (use binoculars if needed)
  • Test water pressure and drainage in all bathrooms and kitchen
  • Check for cracks in walls, ceilings, and foundations
  • Document everything with photos - you'll forget details later
  • Use Property Inspector's tooltips to know what to look for

Critical Questions to Ask

  • Why is the property being sold? (Truth reveals motivation)
  • Age of electrical wiring, plumbing, and geyser?
  • Any building alterations without municipal approval?
  • Average monthly utility costs for electricity and water?
  • Any known defects, past claims, or required repairs?
  • For sectional title: body corporate financial health and pending levies
  • When was roof last repaired and by whom?

Red Flags - Walk Away or Negotiate Hard

  • Fresh paint covering wall cracks or water stains
  • Musty smells indicating mould or long-term damp
  • Sloping or uneven floors (foundation issues)
  • Old electrical panels, exposed wiring, or illegal connections
  • Rushed viewings or seller pressure tactics
  • Reluctance to answer questions or provide documentation
  • Recent "quick fixes" that look poorly done
  • For complexes: high vacancy rates or visible neglect
