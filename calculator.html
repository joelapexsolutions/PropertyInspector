Understanding Your Property Costs

Transfer Duty

Government tax on property ownership transfer. Progressive rates apply based on purchase price — properties below R1.1M are currently exempt.

  • Rates are progressive and increase with property value
  • Calculated automatically above using 2025 SARS brackets
  • Exempt for properties below the current threshold
  • Brackets reviewed annually by National Treasury

Transfer Attorney Fees

Legal fees for transferring ownership to your name. Covers conveyancing, deeds office registration, and all documentation:

  • Professional fees (scale-based on property value)
  • VAT at current rate
  • Deeds office registration fees
  • Title searches, document preparation and admin
  • Fees increase progressively with property price

Bond Registration Costs

Costs to register your home loan with the Deeds Office (only when financing with a bond):

  • Bond attorney fees (separate from transfer attorney)
  • Deeds office registration fees
  • Bank initiation fee (typically R5,000–R7,000)
  • Document preparation and admin
  • Total typically R25,000–R45,000

Monthly Bond Repayment

Your home loan repayment depends on three factors:

  • Loan amount — purchase price minus deposit
  • Interest rate — linked to the prime lending rate (adjust the slider)
  • Loan term — typically 20–30 years
  • Longer terms mean a lower monthly payment but more total interest paid

Smart Buying Tips

Budget for a 10–20% Deposit

A larger deposit secures better interest rates and reduces your monthly repayment. While 100% bonds exist, a deposit significantly lowers the total interest paid over the loan term.

Don’t Forget Once-Off Costs

Transfer duties, attorney fees, and bond registration are significant once-off expenses many buyers overlook. Use this calculator to budget for them before making an offer.

Monthly Costs Beyond Your Bond

Budget for rates, water, electricity, levies (for complexes), insurance, and maintenance. These ongoing costs significantly impact affordability and vary by property and location.

The 30% Rule of Thumb

Total housing costs (bond + rates + levies + insurance) shouldn’t exceed 30% of gross monthly income. Banks typically lend 3–4× annual salary. Calculate affordability before you search.
